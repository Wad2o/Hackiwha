Hook psychology: the specificity rule (most repeated principle in the corpus)

The single most repeated principle across all analyzed discussions:

- Speak to one single person rather than an audience.
- "I tried something" (weak) vs "I tracked every dollar I spent for 30 days"
  (strong).
- "I spent $X and learned Y" beats a generic "How to..." framing by roughly
  4x in engagement rate.
- An "Ultra-Specific Promise" averages 12,100 views with 68% 3-second
  retention, versus 2,400 views and 18% 15-second retention for a generic
  question.

Operational rule: between a generic hook and a hyper-specific hook on the
same topic, always prefer the version containing a number, a sensory
detail, or an ultra-precise situation over a broad category.
