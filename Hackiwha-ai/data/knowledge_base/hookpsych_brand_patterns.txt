Hook psychology: brand-level hook patterns (3 families)

- Duolingo: pattern interrupt — the mascot does something unexpected in the
  first 2 seconds.
- Apple: visual contrast — clean, minimal opening, the eye locks onto the
  product immediately.
- Ryanair: self-deprecating honesty — openly admits its flaws ("our flights
  are cheap, don't expect luxury").
- Starbucks: sensory association — warm, cozy atmosphere established before
  the logo even appears.
- Nike: immediate relatability — shows someone struggling, empathetic
  register rather than aspirational.
- Tesla: statistic interrupt — opens on a fact that challenges common
  thinking before showing the product.
- Red Bull: in medias res entry — no intro, viewer is dropped straight into
  extreme action.

These 7 patterns group into 3 psychological responses: pattern break
(Duolingo, Red Bull), emotional resonance (Starbucks, Nike, Ryanair), and
curiosity gap (Tesla, Apple).
