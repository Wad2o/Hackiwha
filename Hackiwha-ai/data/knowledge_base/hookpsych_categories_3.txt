Hook psychology, part 3: Timeframe Tension, Honest Admission, Controversial Gotcha, Pattern Interrupt, Money-Specific

Timeframe Tension: short or unexpected deadlines trigger a curiosity loop
and hope of attainability. Present in almost every "growth story" hook.

Honest Admission / Vulnerability: counters "polished guru" fatigue,
self-deprecation becomes the hook. Must sound spontaneous, not rehearsed.

Controversial "Gotcha": a shocking claim followed by a hypothetical reveal.
Mixed community reception, close to ragebait — some dislike it while
admitting it works.

Pattern Interrupt (brand): an unexpected sound, visual, or claim breaks the
scroll before the message is even understood.

Money-Specific Hook: a precise dollar amount beats generic educational
framing, roughly 4x in one cited test.
