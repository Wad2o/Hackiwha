Hook psychology, community debate: from "guru" hooks to human-sounding hooks

Multiple sources converge on one point: today's best hooks sound like
genuine, well-articulated human moments, not something overtly "marketed".
Informal test cited: if the hook sounds like a dinner-table conversation,
people lean in; the moment it sounds like an ad, they drop off.

Related: the right balance between over-polished and over-fake speech — a
precisely targeted vulnerability — is what breaks down strangers' distrust
online. But once a vulnerability pattern gets identified and copied by
everyone, the "performed" versions become noticeable: what lasts over time
are versions that resemble genuine spontaneous thought rather than
calculated vulnerability performance.
