Hook psychology: platform adaptation

- TikTok: raw, fast, chaotic energy. The hook must land on the very first
  word, no warm-up, with a mid-thought interruption effect. Bold text helps
  since many watch muted. Jump cuts in the first second signal dynamism to
  the algorithm.
- Instagram Reels: more polished, aspirational energy. About 0.5s more
  tolerance before a swipe. The first aesthetic frame matters more.
  Transformation and POV formats overperform, matching the platform's
  aspirational culture.
- YouTube Shorts: a slightly longer setup is tolerated; a two-sentence hook
  can work where TikTok demands one sentence. Retention is heavily rewarded
  by the algorithm. Curiosity gap and authority challenge work particularly
  well since the audience is used to more educational short-form content.
