Hook psychology, community debate: the hook explains the spike, not sustained growth

A viral hook can explain a one-off view spike without explaining an
account's long-term growth. Cited example: one account plateaued around 48k
followers after a viral hook, while another kept growing to about 98k. This
suggests the quality of content following the winning hook matters at least
as much as the hook itself for long-term growth.

Related: accounts whose topic is literally "how to grow on social media"
are among today's best-performing niches, often starting from zero and
exploding by teaching growth itself — a self-referential effect worth
remembering so as not to over-generalize their results to other niches.
