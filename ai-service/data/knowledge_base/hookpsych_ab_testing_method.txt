Hook psychology: A/B testing method for hooks

1. Record the underlying video footage only once.
2. Edit three different hooks, each from a different psychological category.
3. Post 24-48h apart, or on different platforms.
4. Compare retention curves — most platforms show exactly where people drop
   off; a weak hook produces a "cliff" in the first 2 seconds.
5. Never re-upload the exact same video file cross-platform: platforms
   detect duplicates and suppress reach. Re-filming the hook, even from a
   slightly different angle, counts as original content.

For manual tracking (no bulk export exists for short-form retention),
recommended columns: date, platform, hook ID, views, retention at 3s/15s/30s.
One cited creator spends 60-110 minutes a day on this.
